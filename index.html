Executive Summary

March 2026 recorded a net operating loss of ₹1,08,13,408 against total revenue of ₹36,40,363, with total operating expenses of ₹1,44,53,771. It is important to contextualise this figure: ₹11,42,773 of expenses — representing 7.9% of the total cost base — relates to legal fees that are one-time and non-recurring in nature. Excluding this exceptional item, the adjusted operating loss stands at ₹96,70,635, which more accurately reflects the ongoing operational structure.

The single most significant cost driver this period was Office & Cleaning expenses at ₹41,11,178 (28.4% of total expenses), followed by Travel at ₹28,71,302 (19.9%) and Marketing at ₹24,09,387 (16.7%). Together these three categories account for 65% of all expenditure and represent the primary areas where targeted optimisation will yield the highest return. Revenue showed positive momentum through the month, with Week 4 delivering ₹13,18,894 — more than double Week 1's contribution.

The three strategic priorities for April 2026 are: revenue acceleration through client pipeline expansion, operational cost review across office, travel and marketing spend, and marketing attribution implementation to ensure the ₹24,09,387 marketing investment is generating measurable returns.

Key Insights with Numbers
01

Expenses Are 3.97× Revenue

For every ₹1 earned, ₹3.97 is spent. Even excluding the one-time legal item, the adjusted ratio is 3.66×. To break even at current expense levels, monthly revenue would need to reach ₹1,44,53,771 — requiring a 4× increase in client billing.

02

Office Ops Cost ₹41.1L — 112% of Revenue

Combined Cleaning and Swiggy expenses of ₹41,11,178 exceed total monthly revenue by 12%. This is the single largest controllable cost category and the highest-impact area for immediate review. A 50% reduction alone saves ₹20.6L/month.

03

Travel Costs ₹28.7L With No Policy Visible

Hotel (₹12.2L), Fuel (₹9.4L), and Uber (₹7.1L) together total ₹28,71,302 — 78.9% of total revenue. Without a formal travel policy and pre-approval process, this category will continue to grow unchecked. A spend cap could save ₹10L–₹15L/month.

04

₹24L in Marketing — Attribution Unknown

Facebook Ads (₹13.8L) and Marketing Ads (₹10.3L) represent 66.2% of total monthly revenue with no visible conversion tracking in the data. Without attribution, it is impossible to determine whether this investment is generating client revenue. This must be measured before April billing.

05

Revenue Growing — Week 4 Up 127% vs Week 1

Weekly revenue grew from ₹5,79,788 in Week 1 to ₹13,18,894 in Week 4 — a 127% increase within the same month. This positive momentum indicates strong commercial activity in the second half of March. Sustaining Week 4 levels consistently would add ₹52L in annualised revenue.

06

Salary at 25.3% of Revenue — A Bright Spot

At ₹9,20,425, salary represents just 25.3% of revenue and 6.4% of expenses — well within healthy benchmarks. This means the business has efficient human capital deployment. The path to profitability runs through revenue growth and operational cost reduction, not headcount.

Strategic Recommendations — April 2026
1

Recommendation 1 — Conduct an Immediate Office & Operations Audit

Cleaning (₹16.3L) and Swiggy (₹16.3L) together cost ₹32.6L — more than 89% of total monthly revenue. We recommend a line-by-line audit of all cleaning contracts and food/beverage spend within 14 days. Introducing vendor renegotiation and a monthly spend cap could realistically reduce this category by 40–50%, saving ₹13L–₹16L/month.

Recommendation 1 — Highest Impact
2

Recommendation 2 — Implement a Travel Policy with Pre-Approval

Travel expenses of ₹28,71,302 (Hotel ₹12.2L + Fuel ₹9.4L + Uber ₹7.1L) have no visible policy structure in the current data. Introducing a tiered approval process — under ₹5,000 self-approved, ₹5,000–₹25,000 manager approval, above ₹25,000 director sign-off — alongside hotel rate caps could reduce this category by ₹8L–₹12L/month.

Recommendation 2 — Cost Reduction
3

Recommendation 3 — Measure Marketing ROI Before Next Billing Cycle

₹24,09,387 in marketing spend requires immediate attribution tracking. Before the next Facebook and Marketing Ads billing cycle, implement UTM tracking and a lead-to-client pipeline log. Set a 30-day conversion window per channel. Any channel unable to demonstrate at least ₹2 in revenue per ₹1 spent should be paused. Potential saving: ₹6L–₹12L/month.
